# Samir-Salem-CS250

How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

It first starts in understanding the need and communicating clearly with the user to make sure the changes are understood and everyone is on the same page. Once the need is defined and the team knows what the acceptance criteria is, the project can be cut down into small parts called user stories. This allows for making sure requirements are met and continuous testing throughout the development to ensure everything works at delivery.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

The approach I take is making sure the stories for the project are tracked and completed correctly. Even before that with the planning and design story points are implemented to determine project size and scope. Creating a mockup of the final outcome is also useful not only to the customer but to the development team as well. Makes communication and expectations clear. Future Agile processes that I hope to incorporate are better communication within the team by creating a judgement free environment and encouraging more collaberation between team members.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

A good team member is one who is willing to help out the team when needed to make sure the team is successful. One who communicates clearly and brings up ideas or solutions that can help the team. Being reliable and open to feedaback is invaluable.
